Jean-Paul Louis Ney

Press Director

For more than 20 years, he has been an hi-tech investigative reporter and war reporter, following conflicts in Africa and the Middle East. Former director of the French Centre for Research on Terrorism (CIReT) from 2003 to 2007, he was trained by Yves Bonnet, former director of the DST (French “FBI”). In 1999 he published a book "Virtual Terrors", an impressive top n°1 investigation on cybercrime, stalking and running after the biggest world hackers, thus he found "The Analyzer" the hacker of the Pentagon (1998), a world scoop with SIPA PRESS and Canal Plus Group.

Earlier 2000’s, following his studies and work, he is recruited by police commissioner Daniel Martin, former boss of the cyber branch of the DST, and OCDE member. Together, they create the “Cybercrime Institute” in Paris, known as the IIHEC (International Institute of Advanced Cybercrime Studies).

Jean-Paul Louis joins Canal Plus group in 2000 where he worked on several business, hi-tech and social long investigations. He was the first French reporter officially invited by the National Security Agency (NSA, Fort Meade) and its engineers from the American Intelligence Community.

In 2005 he worked as a consultant for the Interdepartmental Delegate for Defense Restructuring (DIRD), Jean-Pierre Aubert.

In 2007, French President Nicolas Sarkozy entrusted him with the task of producing a book, a « tribute » to the police officers in charge of protecting high-ranking personalities: the SPHP (French secret service). The book, “Le Dernier Rempart” (the Last Rampart) is published with an exclusive foreword by president Sarkozy.

For years now, he has been working on topics related to the evolution of societies, businesses and their protection, both for television (BFMTV, CNN, AL-JAZEERA, AL-MAYADEEN, i24 ...) and for the press and Internet (France Evening, VSD, Paris-Match ...).

Jean-Paul Louis is also co-founder of IICRAI (International Institute for Coordination and Response to Cyber Attacks) with Christophe Casalegno, Technical Director of the Digital Network Group.

Professor in several business schools and journalism schools, Jean-Paul Louis is now providing strategic press advices and crisis responses (fighting fakenews and disinformation, facing information warfare) to private companies individuals as politicians, lawyers, CEOs, and governments. In a confidential work, Jean-Paul Louis was press advisor for the French Special Forces during five years.

In 2013 he launched a confidential letter: CryptoConfidential, with hundreds of followers.
